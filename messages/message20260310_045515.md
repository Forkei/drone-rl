But we're warm-starting from a model that already solves 8/9. The altitude shaping reward is a small additive signal on top of an already-good policy. 2M steps was probably overkill. **300k–500k would very likely be enough** to see if the fix works on `high_target`.

## Thoughts / advice for next session

1. **Kill SAC v2, relaunch at 300k steps.** ~4 hours instead of 27. If high_target still fails, revisit the approach rather than throwing more compute at it.

2. **If altitude shaping doesn't fix high_target:** The failure mode is the drone descending to z≈0.78m instead of climbing to z=1.8m. The potential-based reward (`prev_dist - curr_dist`) should theoretically drive upward motion since moving up reduces 3D distance. Something in the policy has learned to avoid upward thrust. Consider: (a) a dedicated high-target curriculum phase where targets are always above the drone, or (b) adding a direct z-velocity bonus when z_error > 0.3m.

3. **P3 (DR) is the next most valuable thing** once P1 wraps. Confirms 8/9 robustness under physics perturbations — directly relevant to real-world deployment.

4. **Vision phase readiness:** Pipeline is confirmed working. The main design question before starting: what is the reward signal when the drone doesn't have GPS-style `TARGET_POS`? Options: (a) colored marker in scene with known position fed as ground truth during training, (b) detection confidence as reward, (c) depth-to-target. This needs a decision before writing the env.

5. **The 3.0m generalization gap** is a known limitation. If the project ever needs range beyond 2.0m, either: extend training to 3.0m range, or add a 3.0m curriculum stage (like the staged PPO approach that worked well).
