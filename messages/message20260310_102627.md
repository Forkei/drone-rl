## The real problem and options

The fine-tuning approach is fundamentally broken without replay buffer preservation.
SAC supports this natively: model.save_replay_buffer() / model.load_replay_buffer().

**Option 2 (recommended): Replay buffer preservation for fine-tuning**
Save the replay buffer from the golden model's training run, reload it before
fine-tuning. The buffer contains 1M transitions from clean NavAviary — this anchors
the policy while it also sees altitude-specific episodes. This is the correct way
to warm-start SAC.
Problem: we didn't save the replay buffer during original training. Would need to
re-run the original 1M SAC training with buffer saving enabled, then fine-tune.

**Option 1: Train SAC from scratch with altitude curriculum baked in**
Modify NavAviary to include pure-vertical targets in the training distribution
(e.g., 20% of episodes sample altitude-only targets). No warm-start needed.
This avoids catastrophic forgetting entirely. ETA: ~14 hours.

**Option 3: Accept 8/9, move to vision phase**
high_target is a pure edge case (zero XY error). In real deployment, the drone
would rarely face a pure-vertical target with perfect XY alignment. VisionNavAviary
is ready. SAC + CnnPolicy is confirmed compatible. Vision training can start now.

**Option 4: Diagnose PID upward-thrust further**
The oscillating action_z suggests the PID inner loop may not be responding
correctly to upward waypoints near hover equilibrium. Worth understanding before
spending more compute.
