## Root cause: value function mismatch (again, different form)

Same clip_fraction=0.58 throughout all 2M DR training steps.
In v2/v2b attempts: changing REWARD function broke V(s) calibration.
In DR: changing DYNAMICS breaks it too. V(s) was trained on clean physics.
Under wind, the drone visits states outside its training distribution.
Advantage estimates A = G' - V(s) are wrong from step 1 → PPO clips
everything → policy degrades rather than adapts.

Best clean eval in 2M steps: 0.37 (vs v1 peak 12.58). Never recovered.

## The pattern across all three failed attempts:
  1. v2 (vel_penalty at 0.3m): clip_fraction=0.59 throughout, collapse
  2. v2b (vel_penalty in warm_zone): clip_fraction=0.58 throughout, collapse
  3. DR from best_model: clip_fraction=0.58 throughout, degradation

All three share the same failure mode: resumed from v1 best_model into a
changed optimization landscape (reward OR dynamics), value function never
recovers within the training budget. This is a known PPO limitation when
fine-tuning into a different distribution.

## The fix that works: train DR from scratch

Don't resume from the clean best_model. Train DRNavAviary with the full
curriculum from a fresh policy init. The value function builds correctly
on the DR distribution from the start.

Cost: ~50 min for full curriculum on DRNavAviary.
Benefit: no distribution shift, V(s) calibrated correctly from scratch.

Alternatively (more elegant): **curriculum DR warmup**
  - Start with wind_max=0.0001N (nearly imperceptible)
  - Every 100k steps: double wind_max until 0.003N
  - This gives V(s) time to adapt before full perturbation
