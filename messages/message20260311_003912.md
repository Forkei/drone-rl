- Gymnasium-compatible, same obs/reward structure as NavAviary

- Action: 4 motor thrusts [-1, 1]
- 50 Hz control (timestep=0.002, n_substeps=10), 500-step episodes

## FPS Benchmark Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Env FPS (random policy, single env) | **8,633 steps/sec** |
| PyBullet baseline | ~40 steps/sec |
| Env speedup | **216×** |
| Training FPS (SAC MLP, single env, GPU) | 54 steps/sec |
| Training speedup vs PyBullet | 1.3× |

**Key insight:** The env is 216× faster, but `train_freq=1` with a single env means SAC gradient updates bottleneck training back to ~54 fps. Real speedup requires vectorized envs.

## Migration Effort: NavAviary → MuJoCo
**Estimate: 3-5 days** for a working equivalent of our KIN policy:
- HoverEnv is already ~80% of NavAviary (1 day done)
- Need: domain randomization, proper episode termination conditions, curriculum (0.5d)
- Need: train SAC longer (500k+ steps) to get stable flight (not blocking, just time)
- NavAviary has 60-dim obs (includes PID internals); HoverEnv is 16-dim (cleaner)
- No equivalent of ActionType.PID in MuJoCo — direct motor control only (harder for policy to learn but more realistic)

## The Real Play: Vectorized Envs + MJX
With single env: 54 fps (barely faster than PyBullet).
With 64× SubprocVecEnv: ~3,500 fps (estimated).
With **MuJoCo MJX** (GPU physics): potentially 100k+ fps on current hardware.

MJX runs the entire physics loop in JAX on GPU — this is the real migration target. Would need Brax/MJX-based training instead of SB3, but the payoff is enormous.

## Recommendation
1. **Short term:** Use current HoverEnv + more training time (500k-1M steps, SubprocVecEnv×8) to get a working hover policy. Confirm MuJoCo physics matches our needs.
2. **Medium term:** Investigate MJX for GPU-parallel physics — this is the real speedup path.
3. **Vision in MuJoCo:** Camera API is clean and easy. RGB frames from drone-mounted cam work out of the box. Vision policy in MuJoCo is more feasible than PyBullet (no ctrl_freq/IMG_CAPTURE_FREQ hack needed).
