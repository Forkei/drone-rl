## Failure mode analysis (from 3D trajectories + distance curves)

**1. Directional asymmetry**
`approach_-Y` succeeds with a clean monotonic approach. `+X`, `-X`, `+Y` all fail with curved/looping paths.
Looking at the 3D trajectories: approach_+X overshoots badly (the path arcs past the target before curling back),
while approach_-X shows a tight orbit that circles the target for 300 steps without landing.
Root cause: likely asymmetric target distribution during training. Uniform sphere sampling biases the
-Y axis at the 0.3-2.0m range under the home_pos=[0,0,1] convention, or the PID inner controller
has a heading preference. Can't confirm without DR which will expose this clearly.

**2. Approach learned, settling not**
`approach_-X` distance curve: crosses below 0.15m (warm zone) multiple times across 300 steps but
bounces back out each time. The policy learned "fly toward target" but not "decelerate and hold."
`near_0.3m` shows the same — starts 0.3m away, oscillates 0.1-0.5m for 302 steps.
The warm_zone_bonus (0.5/step when dist<0.15m) wasn't enough to overcome the momentum overshoot.
This is a reward/architecture issue, not just a DR issue.

**3. Large initial distance → clean approach; small initial distance → oscillation**
`far_1.8m`: smooth monotonic drop from 1.8m → 0.08m in 40 steps. The policy had a strong
potential-based signal and converged directly.
`near_0.3m`: chaotic oscillation from the start. With small distance, the potential signal
(prev_dist - curr_dist) * 2.0 is tiny and noisy — the policy loses its gradient signal.

**4. Altitude changes harder than horizontal**
Both altitude test cases fail at ~0.8-1.0m from goal. The z-action clamp in step()
([0.15, 2.0]) is probably fine, but the policy may have underfit vertical navigation
because the training curriculum sphere placed equal probability on all directions, and
horizontal movement is less penalized by the crash truncation conditions.

**5. Overshoot then timeout**
`approach_+X` ends in 65 steps — this is a truncation from crashing (excessive tilt or
going out of bounds), not a timeout. The drone overshoots the target, tips over, crashes.
Clear policy instability at the end of the episode.

## My read: two distinct problems
1. **Settling problem**: policy oscillates near goal instead of stopping. This needs either
   a velocity-penalty term in the reward, or a larger warm_zone_bonus, or both.
2. **Directional bias**: probably fixable by training longer, but DR will help by
   diversifying the effective approach distribution.
