## What was done

### Option A: Value Head Reset — FAILED
Reset mlp_extractor.value_net + value_net, froze policy, trained 50k at lr=1e-5, then
unfroze for 200k at lr=3e-5. Results:
- Phase 1: clip_fraction=0.000 (correct — policy frozen)
- Phase 2: clip_fraction=0.586 on the VERY FIRST policy update after unfreeze
- std grew 1.155 -> 1.22 (ent_coef=0.01 actively maintaining entropy)

Conclusion: The problem is the policy gradient itself, not V(s). No fine-tuning from
v1 best_model is possible.

Note: SB3's lr_schedule silently overrides a manually rebuilt optimizer — Phase 2
ran at lr=3e-4 instead of intended lr=3e-5. Not fatal since the fundamental issue
was clip_fraction=0.586 regardless.

## Thoughts for next

The pattern is clear: the 2.0m approach task specifically triggers PPO clipping
(0.66-0.69 in Option D, 0.59 in v1). The policy needs a fundamentally different
approach to learn large-range navigation.

Three viable directions:

1. **More budget for stage4**: Resume `ppo_nav_entropy_final` on fixed 2.0m range
   with ~1-2M more steps. The clip_fraction at 0.66-0.69 is high but the std=0.34
   is workable. v1 spent ~1M steps on 2.0m; Option D only got ~1.2M on it.
   Fix eval_env to use target_range=2.0m when in stage4.

2. **Lower lr in stage4**: The PPO clipping at 2.0m suggests the gradient steps
   are still too large. Try lr=3e-5 (10x reduction) for stage4 only.

3. **Accept the near-range result**: near_0.3m now works (0.091m). Consider
   whether the use case can be served by a 1.2m-range policy from stage3.
   Stage3 checkpoint got approach_+X at 0.204m — not far from success.

The models are in `models_nav_entropy/`. All 4 stage checkpoints + final + best_model
are saved. The training script is `train_nav_entropy.py`.
